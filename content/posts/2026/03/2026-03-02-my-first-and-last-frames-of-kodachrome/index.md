In 1982, after I got my first real film camera (a Canon AE-1 Program), I shot Kodachrome for a short time. Here's the first frame with the new camera. It's a flower in our back yard.

{{< figure src="flower.webp" width= "400px" caption="Flower (1982). Canon AE-1 Program / Kodachrome" alt="Close up image of a pink flower" align="center" >}}

I later switched to using color negative film, and only used Kodachrome occasionally. I don't remember why. Probably because "normal" film was cheaper and prints were 2 for 1 or something.

Kodak stopped making Kodachrome in 2009 and Dwayne's Photo, the only place that still processed it, accepted their last roll at the end of 2010. I wanted to get at a few rolls in before it was too late.

{{< figure src="kodachrome.jpg" width= "800px" caption="My last order of Kodachrome" alt="A small bag containing boxes of Kodachrome" align="center" >}}

I sent them my final roll for processing near the end of 2010, just under the wire. Here's the final frame.

{{< figure src="tractor.webp" width= "800px" caption="Tractor (2010). Olympus OM-1n / Kodachrome" alt="Red tractor in field" align="center" >}}

I miss that film.
